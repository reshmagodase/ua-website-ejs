Fixed Price Strategy

Fixed Price Strategy


How does Fixed Price Procurement Work?
In a Fixed Price Strategy, your total requirement for energy is purchased in one single transaction. To strike the best possible price for you, timing is key. As such, we only fix the price after receiving notification from our team of analysts, who are tracking live market movements.

What are the benefits of choosing Fixed Price Procurement?
Opting for a Fixed Price Strategy, guarantees prices for the duration of the contract. Thus, enabling budget certainty going forward. If your organisation requires price transparency, and cannot tolerate price movements up or down, then a Fixed Price Procurement Strategy may well be for you.

Can I bundle my energy with a larger Basket, to obtain lower prices?
Utility Aid manages energy 'baskets' containing thousands of properties across the UK. By grouping the energy consumption together, the basket participants benefit from considerable purchasing power. In some instances, prices are often 10% less than direct renewal prices.

How is the process managed?
We use a framework agreement to invite prospective suppliers to tender for the total volume of the basket. Using best practice methodology, we then filter the bids to drive the greatest value for our basket participants.

Summary of benefits
  • Grouping spend to drive best price
  • Protection from future increases throughout the life of contract
  • Tracking of markets to optimise strike price
  • Contract lengths of 12 months, 24 months & 36 months available

Market Trigger Strategy

Market Trigger Strategy


How does market trigger strategy work?
Your energy consumption is split into blocks, typically of 6 or 12 months duration. When signing the contract to supply, you choose the percentage to be purchased for the first block. This can be 50% or 75%. The procurement of the remaining block will then be determined by the movement in energy prices breaching agreed 'trigger' points.

If energy prices reach these triggers, a quote is generated for the second block.

What are the benefits of choosing Market Trigger Procurement?
Using a trigger strategy allows you to take advantage of fluctuations in wholesale prices whilst providing the organisation with an element of budget certainty

Summary of benefits
  • Wholesale prices
  • Fixed price options
  • Budget certainty
  • Market awareness and insight with live notifications
  • Variable contract lengths typically 12, 24 & 36 months

Portfolio Managed Strategy

Portfolio Managed Strategy


How does Portfolio Managed Strategy work?
A Portfolio Managed Strategy allows organisations that typically don't have large energy consumption, the ability to access wholesale markets. By placing a number of clients collectively into the portfolio, their consumption is 'bundled together' in order to establish the necessary volume to access the wholesale markets.

We manage the portfolio, making relevant purchase decisions throughout the duration of the contract, according to a pre-determined strategy. You still receive an individual contract and will be invoiced by the supplier. All of our portfolios are tendered with suppliers using a framework agreement. Suppliers invited to tender, are chosen after establishing the suitability of their product, service and price offering.

What are the benefits of choosing Portfolio Procurement?
Choosing Portfolio Procurement spreads the pricing risk, by making multiple purchases across the contract duration. This ensures that over the term, the portfolio achieves a reflective market price for each month. This then negates the stepped price variations many organisations face on renewal of fixed-term agreements.

Summary of benefits
  • Aligns your contract start and end dates with thousands of others thus driving economies of scale
  • Wholesale prices
  • Spreads risk and reduces premiums
  • Avoidance of stepped increases at renewal
  • Market awareness and insight with live notifications
  • Variable contract lengths typically 12, 24 & 36 months
  • Available to Half Hourly, Non-half Hourly & Gas Customers
